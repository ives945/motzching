緋紅之葉 Crimson Leaves

因為每天都會帶狗去附近公園散步的緣故，看到這株槭樹（推測應該是槭而非楓，但我認植物的能力實在殘念）在一片綠中紅成這樣反而讓人覺得有些不合時宜的艷麗，不過看著也是看著，趁著假日要出門買東西的空檔就提著相機順勢將它的模樣記錄下來。

其實若不是我家屁狗全年無休每天要散步兩次，我又迷上了攝影，以我的性格大概很難會特別花時間、精力去注意周遭的變化，即便注意到了也未必會想花時間去觀察。整整看了幾年同樣的景致在四季輪轉、繁榮衰敗，只覺得時間這樣籠統且無形的概念就點滴在咫尺，詮釋了生命就是一種週而復始、循環不止且無悲無喜的過程。

我很喜歡日本文化裡關於「侘寂」二字所衍生的意涵，畢竟天地循環自有道理，若不是人將情感寄物託生，世間萬物生滅又怎得悲喜？不過我到底還是個俗人，做不到那個地步，所以偶爾因此感到悲或喜也是在正常不過的事情。

Because of the daily walks with my dog in the nearby park, I happened to come across this maple tree (though I suspect it’s more likely an acer, but my botanical knowledge is rather lacking) standing out in a sea of green with its red leaves. It seemed somewhat out of place, yet captivating at the same time. So, taking advantage of the gap in time while heading out for shopping on a holiday, I grabbed my camera and decided to capture its beauty.

Honestly, if it weren’t for my dog demanding walks twice a day, and my newfound passion for photography, I doubt I’d take much notice of the changes around me. Even if I did, I probably wouldn’t spend much time observing them. After watching the same scenery for years as the seasons come and go, witnessing the cycle of growth and decline, I’ve come to feel that time, that abstract and omnipresent concept, is constantly slipping away, embodying life as a repetitive, cyclical process devoid of sorrow or joy.

I’ve always been drawn to the concept of “wabi-sabi” in Japanese culture. After all, the cycle of nature is inevitable, and without human emotions invested in objects and life, how could there be sorrow or joy in the ever-changing world? But I’m only human, and I can’t quite reach that level of detachment, so occasionally feeling sadness or joy is just part of being normal.
